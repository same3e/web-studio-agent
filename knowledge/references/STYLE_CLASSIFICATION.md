## Migrated legacy style vocabulary

# Style index

Use this as a practical controlled vocabulary for reference classification and project direction. A style family names the dominant visual language; style tags and modifiers describe the specific expression. A label is never permission to harm legibility, accessibility, performance, or conversion.

## Minimalism

- **Core visual principles:** restraint, reduction, whitespace, and a small number of intentional elements.
- **Typography:** one clear sans-serif or restrained serif pairing with a disciplined scale.
- **Composition:** simple grids, calm alignment, and generous negative space.
- **UI treatment:** quiet controls, limited borders, restrained colour and motion.
- **Suitable elements:** premium services, product introductions, proof strips, editorial image blocks.
- **Common risks:** sparse content can hide the offer, CTA, or proof; minimal must not mean empty.
- **Works well for:** professional services, architecture, wellness, premium retail, technology.

## Swiss

- **Core visual principles:** rational hierarchy, strong grid, asymmetry, contrast, and information clarity.
- **Typography:** functional grotesk sans-serif, often large with precise weight and spacing.
- **Composition:** modular columns, visible alignment, bold type/image relationships.
- **UI treatment:** direct controls, graphic rules, restrained palette, purposeful motion.
- **Suitable elements:** data, navigation, service systems, statements, programme listings.
- **Common risks:** can feel cold, dense, or overly institutional when hierarchy is not softened.
- **Works well for:** B2B, finance, culture, technology, architecture, agencies.

## Editorial

- **Core visual principles:** narrative pacing, art direction, contrast between display and reading text.
- **Typography:** expressive serif or display face paired with highly readable body text.
- **Composition:** image-led spreads, asymmetric modules, deliberate rhythm and cropped media.
- **UI treatment:** understated controls, captions, rules, layered type and media.
- **Suitable elements:** storytelling heroes, portfolios, case studies, brand statements.
- **Common risks:** a beautiful story can bury a service offer, CTA, or practical navigation.
- **Works well for:** hospitality, architecture, culture, fashion, premium property, creative studios.

## Brutalism

- **Core visual principles:** raw contrast, intentional friction, exposed structure, and unapologetic scale.
- **Typography:** bold sans-serif, monospace, or deliberately utilitarian type.
- **Composition:** hard grids, collision, high contrast, and simple blocks.
- **UI treatment:** visible borders, stark buttons, limited effects, direct feedback.
- **Suitable elements:** campaigns, portfolios, culture, experimental launches, creative tools.
- **Common risks:** fatigue, inaccessible contrast or motion, and low trust for sensitive services.
- **Works well for:** art, music, culture, agencies, youth brands, experimental technology.

## Soft morphism

- **Core visual principles:** soft depth, rounded layered surfaces, gentle gradients, and mostly non-translucent elevation.
- **Typography:** clean, high-legibility sans-serif with enough contrast against surfaces.
- **Composition:** layered modules, soft shadows, rounded containers, open spacing.
- **UI treatment:** gentle non-translucent elevation, soft gradients, rounded controls, and modest transitions.
- **Suitable elements:** selectors, dashboards, service cards, friendly product panels.
- **Common risks:** weak boundaries, washed-out text, excessive decoration, and slow rendering.
- **Works well for:** wellness, lifestyle, travel, education, consumer products, friendly SaaS.

## Glassmorphism

- **Core visual principles:** translucency and backdrop blur are essential; use them to create depth over luminous backgrounds.
- **Typography:** strong contrast and simple sans-serif type to survive translucent surfaces.
- **Composition:** layered foreground cards over image, gradient, or colour fields.
- **UI treatment:** frosted panels, subtle borders, restrained transparency and motion.
- **Suitable elements:** compact proof cards, selectors, navigation, product-demo overlays.
- **Common risks:** unreadable text, visual noise, performance cost, and a generic “trend” look.
- **Works well for:** technology, creative tools, travel, premium services, selected healthcare accents.

## Neumorphism

- **Core visual principles:** inset or extruded controls on one same-colour surface are essential; light and shadow create the sculpted effect.
- **Typography:** sober, high-contrast type to offset quiet controls.
- **Composition:** spacious, modular, monochrome or near-monochrome layouts.
- **UI treatment:** extruded or inset controls with clear alternate state indicators.
- **Suitable elements:** simple dashboards, calm controls, wellness or device interfaces.
- **Common risks:** poor affordance and contrast; never rely on shadow alone for state or action.
- **Works well for:** wellness, consumer devices, personal tools; use sparingly elsewhere.

## Hyper-typography

- **Core visual principles:** type is the primary compositional and expressive device.
- **Typography:** oversized display type, variable weights, dramatic scale and line breaks.
- **Composition:** type-led hero with media or UI in supporting roles.
- **UI treatment:** minimal chrome, clear CTA contrast, careful overflow control.
- **Suitable elements:** campaign heroes, studio portfolios, launch statements, cultural programmes.
- **Common risks:** offer disappears, translations break layout, and mobile becomes crowded.
- **Works well for:** agencies, fashion, culture, architecture, premium launches, selected services.

## Soft luxury

- **Core visual principles:** tactile calm, measured scarcity, quality cues, and warm refinement.
- **Typography:** elegant serif/display paired with a quiet sans-serif, or refined sans alone.
- **Composition:** generous space, material photography, balanced asymmetry, modest detail.
- **UI treatment:** warm neutrals, fine rules, large radii only where purposeful, subtle motion.
- **Suitable elements:** craftsmanship, hospitality, premium service proof, product detail.
- **Common risks:** vague copy, low contrast, and visual preciousness that weakens conversion.
- **Works well for:** hospitality, beauty, interiors, property, premium healthcare, jewellery.

## Clean corporate

- **Core visual principles:** confidence, clarity, repeatable systems, and low decision friction.
- **Typography:** accessible sans-serif with a stable hierarchy and readable data labels.
- **Composition:** orderly grid, predictable sections, visible proof, and clear CTA paths.
- **UI treatment:** defined buttons, useful cards, restrained brand colour, robust states.
- **Suitable elements:** service comparisons, forms, trust panels, case studies, process sections.
- **Common risks:** blandness, card overload, and generic claims disguised as professionalism.
- **Works well for:** B2B, finance, insurance, clinics, logistics, consulting, SaaS.

## Premium dark

- **Core visual principles:** cinematic contrast, controlled glow, depth, and focused attention.
- **Typography:** luminous light type with ample size and line-height; avoid thin small text.
- **Composition:** deep fields, selective imagery, dramatic focal zones, spacious rhythm.
- **UI treatment:** dark surfaces, carefully tested contrast, restrained gradients and animation.
- **Suitable elements:** launches, high-end product pages, portfolios, immersive case studies.
- **Common risks:** inaccessible contrast, heavy imagery, poor daylight usability, and weak trust for care services.
- **Works well for:** technology, automotive, entertainment, fashion, premium property, creative studios.

## Airy light

- **Core visual principles:** brightness, openness, breathing room, and clear, optimistic hierarchy.
- **Typography:** crisp sans-serif or soft serif display with comfortable reading sizes.
- **Composition:** light backgrounds, wide gaps, image or colour moments with clear anchors.
- **UI treatment:** light palette, gentle borders, simple controls, restrained elevation.
- **Suitable elements:** consultation flows, service overviews, product categories, testimonials.
- **Common risks:** insufficient contrast, washed-out CTAs, and excess whitespace on mobile.
- **Works well for:** healthcare, wellness, education, home, travel, consumer services.
