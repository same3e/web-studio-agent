## Migrated legacy copy examples

# Copy examples

These are structural patterns, not approved claims or text to copy. Use them only when the business facts support them.

## Result-led headline

Name the service or object and a usable client result: “Prepare a kitchen project and estimate for your budget.”

## Calculation or selection offer

Offer a concrete first-step output: “Select a system for the site and calculate installation cost.”

## Time-bound offer

State a time only when verified: “Prepare a preliminary estimate within 20 minutes.”

## Fixed-price or risk-reversal offer

State a fixed price, free visit, or guarantee only when the condition is real and documented.

## Proof close to CTA

Place verified facts beside the CTA: named process stages, own production, real review source, confirmed qualification, or a documented guarantee.

## Brand-led exception

For a portfolio or editorial project, an evocative headline is acceptable only after the service or product is clear in nearby copy and CTA.

## Bad abstract alternatives

“New level of quality” and “Care built on precision” communicate mood, not offer, result, or next step. Do not use them as standalone conversion-first H1s.

## Before/after rewrite

Abstract: “Care built on precision.”

More concrete only if the business confirms it: “Complete a diagnostic visit and receive a clear treatment plan at your first consultation.”

Supporting copy: “The doctor explains options, timing, and preliminary cost so you can decide without pressure.”

CTA: “Book an initial consultation.”

This is an example of structure, not a verified claim about any clinic.
